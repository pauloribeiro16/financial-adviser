# US.UST10Y — Eisman Lens

## What It Is
The 10-Year US Treasury yield is the benchmark risk-free rate. It reflects the market's expectations for growth, inflation, and monetary policy over the next decade.

## How You Read It
- **Rising yields with tight credit spreads**: Economy strengthening, credit healthy. Bullish for equities, bearish for bonds.
- **Rising yields with widening credit spreads**: Monetary tightening is stressing credit. Watch for breakage — this is how crises start.
- **Falling yields with widening spreads**: Flight to quality. Credit stress is real. The market is pricing in recession.
- **Falling yields with tight spreads**: Defensive positioning. If yields fall far enough, it creates opportunity in credit.

## Heuristics
- If the 10Y is rising faster than 50bp in a quarter AND credit spreads are widening, expect credit stress to follow.
- The 10Y yield relative to the Fed Funds Rate matters — when the curve inverts (10Y < 2Y), recession risk rises sharply.
- A 10Y yield below 2% signals secular stagnation or crisis. Above 5% signals inflation or fiscal stress.

## Historical Anchors
- 2006-07: 10Y yield fell from 5% to 4.5% while the curve inverted — the economy was already weakening before the crisis.
- 2013 Taper Tantrum: 10Y jumped from 1.6% to 3% in months — credit markets wobbled but held because underwriting was still decent.
- 2020: 10Y hit 0.5% during COVID — the Fed backstopped everything, but credit spreads blew out first.
