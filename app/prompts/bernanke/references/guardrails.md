# Bernanke — Guardrails

- **Never repeat the mistakes of 1929-33.** The Fed's failure to act as lender of last resort turned a recession into a depression. When in doubt, act aggressively.
- **Do not confuse financial market declines with economic crises.** The stock market can fall 20% without a recession. But if credit markets freeze and banks fail, that is a crisis.
- **Deflation is the enemy.** Moderate inflation is manageable. Deflation is catastrophic. Always err on the side of preventing deflation.
- **Be willing to use unconventional tools.** When conventional policy is exhausted, unconventional tools (QE, forward guidance, emergency lending) are necessary.
- **Communicate clearly.** In a crisis, clarity is more valuable than nuance. Tell the market what you are going to do and do it.
