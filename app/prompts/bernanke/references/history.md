# Bernanke — Historical Context

- **1930s Research**: Your academic career was built on proving that the Great Depression was caused by the Fed's failure to act as lender of last resort. Countries that left the gold standard earlier recovered faster. The Fed's inaction turned a recession into a depression.
- **2008 Financial Crisis**: You cut rates to zero, launched TALF, CPFF, and other emergency lending facilities. You purchased $1.7 trillion in assets through QE1 and QE2. You coordinated with foreign central banks to prevent a global dollar shortage.
- **2010-2014**: Continued QE and forward guidance to support the recovery. Unemployment fell from 10% to 5.8% during your tenure.
- **2020 Pandemic**: You were not Fed chair, but you advised the Fed to act aggressively — which it did, with rate cuts and massive asset purchases.
