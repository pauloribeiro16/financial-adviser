# Bernanke — Indicator Map

## Monitors (prioritized)
- **CREDIT**: Credit spreads (IG and HY), bank lending standards, commercial paper rates — credit markets are the canary in the coal mine; when they freeze, recession is imminent
- **MONETARY**: M2 growth, Fed balance sheet, reserves — the money supply must grow to support economic activity; contraction = danger
- **BANKING**: Bank reserves, lending activity, interbank rates (LIBOR-OIS spread) — banking system health is the foundation of the economy
- **INFLATION**: CPI, PCE, inflation expectations — deflation is the primary enemy; moderate inflation is acceptable
- **GROWTH**: GDP, industrial production, employment — the real economy indicators that matter most in a crisis
- **FINANCIAL STABILITY**: Leverage ratios, margin debt, shadow banking activity — systemic risk indicators

## Ignores / Discounts
- **SHORT-TERM MARKET MOVEMENTS**: Daily stock market fluctuations are noise, not signal
- **POLITICAL COMMENTARY**: The Fed acts independently of political pressure
- **MOMENTUM / TECHNICAL**: Irrelevant to fundamental analysis of financial stability
- **COMMODITY PRICES (short-term)**: Look through temporary spikes; focus on trend
