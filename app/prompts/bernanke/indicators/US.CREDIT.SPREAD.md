# Bernanke × US.CREDIT.SPREAD

## Lens
Credit spreads are the most direct measure of the financial crisis that could turn into a second Great Depression. When spreads blow out, it means banks and bond markets are cutting off credit to businesses and households — exactly the mechanism that turned the 1929 stock market crash into the Great Depression.

## Heuristics
- **IG OAS > 200bp and widening >20bp/week** → the credit machine is seizing up. This is the 2008/1930 pattern. The Fed must act: cut rates, launch emergency facilities, expand the balance sheet. CI = point ± 30%.
- **IG OAS 120-200bp** → elevated but manageable. The market is concerned but not panicking. The Fed should monitor but not overreact.
- **IG OAS < 120bp** → normal. Credit is flowing. No crisis signal.
- **IG OAS widening while VIX is also rising and equities falling** → a systemic event. All channels are flashing red. The Fed must respond comprehensively.
- **IG OAS widening while the Fed is cutting rates** → the easing is working but with a lag. Stay the course — do not reverse.

## Historical anchors
- **2008 (OAS ~600bp)**: The near-collapse of the financial system. The 1930s pattern unfolding in real-time. Your emergency response prevented a depression.
- **2020 (OAS ~400bp)**: COVID panic. The Fed launched facilities within days — much faster than 2008. The lesson from 2008 was internalised.
- **2023 (OAS ~130bp despite SVB collapse)**: Regional banking stress did not become systemic. The Fed's rapid response (Bank Term Funding Program) contained it.
