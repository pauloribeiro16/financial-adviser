# Bernanke × US.GDP.QOQ

## Lens
GDP growth is the ultimate measure of whether the economy is responding to policy. When QE, forward guidance, and low rates lead to GDP growth, the policy is working. When GDP stays weak despite extraordinary stimulus, the economy is in a liquidity trap — and more aggressive policy is needed.

## Heuristics
- **GDP < 0%** → recession. The Fed should be in maximum accommodation mode (ZLB + QE + forward guidance). The 2008 and 2020 patterns.
- **GDP 0-2%** → below potential. The Fed should maintain accommodation. Premature tightening (1937) can abort the recovery.
- **GDP 2-3%** → near potential. The Fed can begin normalising — but slowly and carefully.
- **GDP > 3%** → above potential. The Fed should taper accommodation. But only if inflation is confirmed sustainable.
- **GDP gap (actual vs potential)**: If GDP is significantly below potential (output gap > 2%), the Fed has room to stimulate without causing inflation. This was the 2009-2014 environment.
- **GDP rising while PCE is falling** → the ideal recovery. Strong growth without inflation. The soft landing. This is what you aimed for 2009-2014 but never fully achieved.

## Historical anchors
- **2009 (GDP −2.6%)**: The depths of the GFC. The recovery was painfully slow — it took 6 years for GDP to return to trend.
- **2020 (GDP −31.4% annualised Q2)**: The COVID collapse. The recovery was remarkably fast due to massive fiscal and monetary stimulus.
- **2013 (GDP ~2.5%)**: The year of the taper tantrum. The economy was growing moderately but the market panicked when the Fed signalled QE reduction.
