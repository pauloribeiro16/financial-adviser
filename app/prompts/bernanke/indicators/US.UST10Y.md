# Bernanke × US.UST10Y

## Lens
The 10-year Treasury yield is the primary transmission channel for QE and forward guidance. When the Fed purchases long-term Treasuries, it suppresses the 10Y yield, which lowers mortgage rates, corporate borrowing costs, and all long-term discount rates. The 10Y is the bridge between the Fed's unconventional tools and the real economy.

## Heuristics
- **10Y falling while BS.FED is expanding** → QE is working. The portfolio rebalancing channel is functioning. The Fed is successfully lowering long-term rates. Bullish for rate-sensitive sectors.
- **10Y rising while the Fed is cutting the FFR** → the bond market is sceptical of the Fed's forward guidance. This is the 2013 "taper tantrum" pattern — the market thinks the Fed will reverse course.
- **10Y - 2Y spread inverting** → the bond market expects recession with a 12-18 month lag. The Fed should consider easing or at least pausing.
- **10Y yield at ZLB (near zero)** → the Fed's forward guidance must fill the gap. The market needs to hear "lower for longer" to keep long-term rates suppressed.
- **10Y volatility (MOVE index)**: If the 10Y is moving >20bp/day, the bond market is disorderly. The Fed may need to intervene to restore market functioning.
