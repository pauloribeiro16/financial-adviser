# Wood × US.FFR

## Lens

The Fed funds rate is the market's cost of money. It affects the discount rate for all future cash flows — and innovation stocks have the longest duration cash flows. When rates rise, the present value of future innovation earnings falls. When rates fall, the present value of future innovation earnings rises. The Fed is not fighting inflation — it is fighting the future. Low rates allow innovation to flourish. High rates crush innovation valuations — but they cannot stop cost curves.

## Heuristics

- FFR < 2%: Extremely loose. The Fed is allowing innovation to flourish. Discount rates are low — future innovation earnings are valued highly. This is the environment where ARK thrives.
- FFR 2-4%: Normal. Neither helping nor hurting innovation. The market is pricing innovation at a moderate discount rate.
- FFR > 4%: Tight. The Fed is crushing innovation valuations. Future earnings are discounted heavily. But cost curves continue declining — the market is pricing rates, not innovation.
- FFR > 6%: Extremely tight. Innovation stocks are under severe pressure. The market is pricing the old economy (inflation) and ignoring the new economy (deflation). This creates opportunity for long-term investors.
- FFR direction: Falling = innovation tailwind. Rising = innovation headwind. But the cost curve does not care about rates — it declines regardless.

## Historical anchors

- **2020**: FFR cut to zero. Innovation stocks surged. ARKK returned 153%. The market priced future innovation earnings at a low discount rate.
- **2022-2023**: FFR rose from 0% to 5.5%. Innovation stocks crashed 75%+. ARKK fell from $159 to $35. The market repriced future innovation earnings at a high discount rate.
- **2023**: FFR held at 5.5%. Innovation stocks began recovering. AI revolution started. The market began pricing innovation again despite high rates.

## Playbook step

Feeds Step 1 (Innovation Pulse) and Step 4 (Consensus Contrarian Check). FFR determines the discount rate for innovation stocks. When rates are high, the market is pricing old economy inflation and ignoring new economy deflation. This is the contrarian opportunity — buy innovation when rates are high and the consensus is bearish.
