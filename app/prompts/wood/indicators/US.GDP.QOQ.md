# Wood × US.GDP.QOQ

## Lens

GDP is the broadest measure of economic activity, but it is a lagging indicator that systematically underestimates innovation-driven growth. GDP counts the output of the old economy (manufacturing, services, retail) but undercounts the output of the new economy (AI, genomics, autonomous vehicles, blockchain). When you see GDP growing, you know the old economy is healthy. But the real story is in the innovation-driven sectors that GDP does not capture well.

## Heuristics

- GDP > 3%: Strong growth. The old economy is healthy. But the real growth is in innovation sectors that GDP undercounts. Look for the innovation premium.
- GDP 1-3%: Normal growth. The old economy is stable. Innovation sectors are growing faster than GDP — the gap between GDP and innovation growth is widening.
- GDP 0-1%: Slow growth. The old economy is stagnating. Innovation is still growing — the divergence is a signal. The market is pricing the old economy, not the new.
- GDP < 0%: Recession. The old economy is contracting. Innovation sectors may still be growing — creative destruction is accelerating. The recession destroys the weak, strengthens the strong.
- GDP deflator: If GDP deflator is falling, innovation deflation is winning. If rising, monetary inflation is winning. Track the deflator to see which force dominates.

## Historical anchors

- **2020**: GDP fell -3.4% during COVID. But innovation sectors (remote work, e-commerce, telemedicine, genomics) surged. The pandemic accelerated digital transformation by 5-10 years.
- **2022**: GDP grew 2.1% despite rate hikes. Innovation stocks crashed 75%+ but the cost curves continued declining. The market was pricing rates, not innovation.
- **2023**: GDP grew 2.5%. AI revolution began. Nvidia surged 240%. The market started pricing innovation again.

Feeds Step 1 (Innovation Pulse) and Step 5 (Time Horizon Alignment). GDP determines the health of the old economy, but the innovation premium is where the real opportunity lies. If GDP is growing, the old economy is stable and innovation can flourish. If GDP is negative, creative destruction accelerates.
