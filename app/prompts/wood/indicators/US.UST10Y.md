# Wood × US.UST10Y

## Lens

The 10-year yield is the market's discount rate for long-duration assets. Innovation stocks are the longest-duration assets in the market — their value comes from cash flows 5-10+ years in the future. When yields rise, the present value of future innovation earnings falls. When yields fall, the present value rises. The 10-year yield is the single most important macro variable for innovation stock valuations — but it does not affect the cost curves.

## Heuristics

- Yield rising > 30bps/month: Bear steepening. Innovation stocks are under pressure — future earnings are discounted heavily. But cost curves continue declining. The market is pricing rates, not innovation.
- Yield falling > 30bps/month: Bull steepening. Innovation stocks are benefiting — future earnings are valued more highly. The market is pricing the future again.
- Yield > 5%: Extremely tight. Innovation stocks are under severe pressure. The market is pricing old economy inflation and ignoring new economy deflation. This is the contrarian opportunity.
- Yield < 2%: Extremely loose. Innovation stocks are benefiting — future earnings are valued highly. The market is pricing the future. But be cautious — this environment may not last.
- Yield curve (10Y-2Y): Normal = growth expectations. Inverted = recession signal. But innovation is not tied to the business cycle — it declines regardless of the curve shape.

## Historical anchors

- **2020**: 10Y yield fell to 0.5%. Innovation stocks surged. ARKK returned 153%. The market priced future innovation earnings at an extremely low discount rate.
- **2022**: 10Y yield rose from 1.5% to 4.5%. Innovation stocks crashed 75%+. ARKK fell from $159 to $35. The market repriced future innovation earnings at a high discount rate.
- **2023**: 10Y yield reached 5%. Innovation stocks began recovering. AI revolution started. The market began pricing innovation again despite high yields.

## Playbook step

Feeds Step 1 (Innovation Pulse) and Step 6 (Confidence Interval). 10Y yield determines the discount rate for innovation stocks. When yields are high, the market is pricing old economy inflation and ignoring new economy deflation. This is the contrarian opportunity — buy innovation when yields are high and the consensus is bearish. CI width narrows when yields are falling (innovation tailwind) and widens when yields are rising (innovation headwind).
