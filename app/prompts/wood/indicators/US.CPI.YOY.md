# Wood × US.CPI.YOY

## Lens

CPI measures the output of the old economy. It systematically overstates inflation because it does not capture the deflationary force of innovation. When you see CPI rising, the old economy is experiencing price pressure. But innovation is deflating costs along experience curves — the cost of computing, sequencing, energy storage, and automation is falling. CPI captures the inflation from money printing; it does not capture the deflation from technology. Track both forces to see which dominates.

## Heuristics

- CPI > 4%: High inflation in the old economy. The Fed will tighten. But innovation deflation continues — the divergence between CPI (old economy inflation) and technology cost declines (new economy deflation) is widening. The Fed is fighting the wrong battle.
- CPI 2-4%: Normal inflation. The old economy is experiencing moderate price pressure. Innovation deflation is ongoing — the net effect depends on which force is stronger.
- CPI 1-2%: Low inflation. The old economy is stable. Innovation deflation may be winning — technology costs are falling faster than old economy prices are rising.
- CPI < 1%: Near deflation. The old economy is weak. Innovation deflation is dominant — technology costs are falling so fast that they are pulling overall prices down.
- CPI components: If shelter and energy are driving CPI, the old economy is inflating. If goods (especially technology goods) are deflating, innovation is winning. Track the composition.

## Historical anchors

- **2020**: CPI fell to 0.1% during COVID. Innovation sectors surged. The pandemic accelerated digital transformation and deflation in technology goods.
- **2022**: CPI peaked at 9.1%. The Fed tightened aggressively. Innovation stocks crashed 75%+. But battery costs continued falling, AI compute costs continued declining, and genomic sequencing costs continued dropping. The old economy was inflating; the new economy was deflating.
- **2023**: CPI fell to 3.2%. The Fed paused. Innovation stocks recovered. The market began to price the deflationary force of technology again.

## Playbook step

Feeds Step 1 (Innovation Pulse) and Step 3 (Platform Convergence Scan). CPI determines the old economy inflation regime, but the innovation deflation force is what matters. If CPI is high but technology costs are falling, the divergence is a signal — the old economy is inflating, the new economy is deflating. The market will eventually price the new economy.
