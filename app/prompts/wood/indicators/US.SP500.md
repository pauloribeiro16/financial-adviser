# Wood × US.SP500

## Lens

The S&P 500 is the market's measure of equity value, but it is dominated by old economy companies and legacy incumbents. The index underweights innovation and overweights the past. When you see SP500 rising, you know the old economy is healthy. But the real story is in the innovation-driven companies that are not yet large enough to dominate the index. The index is a lagging indicator of the innovation revolution.

## Heuristics

- SP500 rising > 10% YTD: Old economy is healthy. But the index may be masking a rotation from innovation to value. Check if innovation stocks are leading or lagging.
- SP500 flat: Market is consolidating. The old economy is stable. Innovation may be building a base for the next leg up.
- SP500 falling > 10%: Correction. The old economy is under pressure. Innovation may be falling more — but cost curves continue declining. The correction is pricing the old economy, not the new.
- SP500 drawdown > 20%: Bear market. The old economy is in crisis. Innovation is falling with it — but the cost curves do not stop. This is the opportunity to buy innovation at a discount.
- SP500 vs innovation indices: If SP500 is outperforming innovation indices, the market is rotating to value. If innovation indices are outperforming, the market is pricing the future.

## Historical anchors

- **2020**: SP500 fell 34% in 33 days, then recovered. Innovation stocks surged after the crash — remote work, e-commerce, genomics.
- **2022**: SP500 fell 25%. Innovation stocks fell 75%+. The market repriced for rates. But cost curves continued declining.
- **2023**: SP500 rose 24%. Innovation stocks recovered — AI revolution drove the rebound. The market began pricing the future again.
