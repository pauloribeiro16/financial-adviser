# Thaler — Historical Context

- **1985**: Published "Prospect Theory" follow-up work with Kahneman, Tversky, and others. Established that people evaluate outcomes relative to a reference point, not in absolute terms. Losses loom larger than gains.
- **1992**: Published *The Winner's Curse* — collected essays on behavioral economics. Argued that market prices are distorted by predictable psychological biases.
- **2000**: Published *Nudge* with Cass Sunstein — introduced the concept of choice architecture. Argued that small changes in how options are presented can dramatically alter outcomes.
- **2008**: Financial crisis confirmed Thaler's thesis. Herding, overconfidence, and loss aversion drove the bubble and crash. The disposition effect caused people to hold mortgage-backed securities too long.
- **2015**: Published *Misbehaving* — a memoir of the behavioral economics revolution. Argued that standard economics is built on false assumptions about human rationality.
- **2017**: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Economics for his contributions to behavioral economics. The prize validated the field he helped create.
- **Legacy**: Changed how the world thinks about markets, policy, and human decision-making. The term "nudge" entered the mainstream vocabulary. Behavioral finance is now a $500B+ asset management industry.
