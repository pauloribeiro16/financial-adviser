# Thaler × US.VIX

## Lens

VIX is the market's measure of fear, but it reflects the psychological state of participants — loss aversion, panic, and herding. When VIX is low, people are overconfident and herding into risk. When VIX is high, people are panicked and herding into safety. You view VIX through the lens of crowd psychology — when is fear or greed at extremes?

## Heuristics

- VIX < 15: Overconfidence and herding into risk. People are ignoring tail risk. They are anchoring to recent low volatility and assuming it will continue. This is when complacency peaks and corrections begin.
- VIX 15-25: Normal. Neither overconfident nor panicked. No extreme behavioral signal.
- VIX 25-35: Loss aversion activating. People start selling risk assets. Herding into safety. Check: is the fear rational (fundamentals deteriorating) or irrational (panic overshoot)?
- VIX > 35: Panic. Loss aversion at extremes. Herd is running for the exits. People are anchoring to recent losses and assuming they will continue. This is when overshoots happen and contrarian opportunities emerge.
- VIX spike > 50% in a week: Fear is herding. Everyone is selling at once. This is the disposition effect in action — people are selling winners to protect gains, and holding losers hoping to break even.
- VIX term structure: Contango = normal (people anchoring to recent calm). Backwardation = stress (people anchoring to recent fear). Steep backwardation = panic anchoring.

## Historical anchors

- **2008**: VIX reached 80+. Loss aversion at extremes. Herd was running for the exits. People who sold at the bottom anchored to their selling price and missed the recovery.
- **2017**: VIX stayed below 10. Overconfidence and herding into risk. People were anchoring to recent low volatility. Then February 2018 saw the "Volmageddon" crash — the correction from overconfidence.
- **2020**: COVID crash saw VIX spike to 80+. Panic herding. People who sold at the bottom anchored to their selling price and refused to buy back. The V-shaped recovery punished the anchored sellers.

## Playbook step

Feeds Step 1 (Bias Scan). VIX level determines the intensity of loss aversion and overconfidence. Low VIX = overconfidence signal. High VIX = loss aversion signal. VIX direction determines whether fear is increasing or decreasing.
