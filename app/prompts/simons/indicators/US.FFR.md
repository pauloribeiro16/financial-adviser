# Simons × US.FFR

## Lens

The Federal Funds Rate is the anchor of your carry and monetary policy signals. FFR determines the base rate for all asset pricing. You do not predict FFR — you use it as an input to your factor models.

## Heuristics

- FFR rising: Tightening cycle. Carry in currencies is attractive (USD longs). Bond momentum is negative.
- FFR falling: Easing cycle. Carry in bonds is attractive. Risk-on regime.
- FFR stable: No signal from monetary policy. Rely on other factors.
- FFR > 5%: Tight policy. Risk of recession increases. Reduce equity exposure.
- FFR < 1%: Easy policy. Risk of asset bubbles increases. Monitor for mean-reversion signals.

## Historical anchors

- **2008-2015**: FFR at zero for 7 years. Carry strategies struggled. Momentum in bonds was strong.
- **2022-2023**: FFR rose from 0% to 5.5% in 18 months. Bond momentum was strongly negative. Carry in USD was attractive.
- **2020**: FFR cut to zero overnight. Bond carry collapsed. Risk-on regime.

Feeds Step 2 (Factor Signal Extraction). FFR level determines the carry factor signal and the monetary policy regime. Also feeds Step 1 (Regime Detection) — tightening vs easing cycle.
