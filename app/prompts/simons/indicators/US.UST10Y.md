# Simons × US.UST10Y

## Lens

The 10-year yield is your primary rate regime signal. It is the benchmark for all asset pricing and reflects the market's expectation of growth, inflation, and monetary policy.

## Heuristics

- Yield rising > 20bps/month: Bear steepening. Risk-off for bonds. Bond momentum is negative.
- Yield falling > 20bps/month: Bull steepening. Risk-on for bonds. Bond momentum is positive.
- Yield curve (10Y-2Y): Normal (positive) = growth expectations. Inverted = recession signal. Steep = recovery.
- Yield level > 5%: Extremely tight. Recession risk increases. Mean-reversion signal may be forming.
- Yield level < 2%: Extremely loose. Risk of asset bubbles. Mean-reversion signal may be forming.

## Historical anchors

- **2020**: 10Y yield fell to 0.5%. Extreme low. Mean-reversion signal was strong.
- **2022**: 10Y yield rose from 1.5% to 4.5%. Bond momentum was strongly negative.
- **2023**: 10Y yield reached 5%. Tight policy. Recession risk signal.
