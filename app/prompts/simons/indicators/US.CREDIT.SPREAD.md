# Simons × US.CREDIT.SPREAD

## Lens

Credit spreads are your primary risk appetite signal. When spreads widen, risk-off. When spreads tighten, risk-on. The level and slope of credit spreads are both signals.

## Heuristics

- Spread < 300bps: Tight credit. Risk-on regime. Favor risk assets.
- Spread 300-500bps: Normal credit conditions. Balanced signals.
- Spread > 500bps: Stress regime. Reduce all positions. Buy protection.
- Spread widening > 50bps in a month: Regime change signal. Check correlation regime.
- Spread tightening > 50bps in a month: Risk-on momentum. Increase risk exposure.
- Spread curve (short vs long): Inverted spread curve = stress. Steep = normal.

## Historical anchors

- **2008**: Credit spreads exceeded 2000bps. Crisis regime — all factor signals were overwhelmed by risk-off.
- **2020**: COVID spike to 1100bps. Brief crisis regime, then rapid tightening.
- **2022**: Spread widened to 500bps during rate hikes. Elevated but not crisis.
