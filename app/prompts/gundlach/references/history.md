# Gundlach — Historical Context

- **2000**: Founded DoubleLine Capital. Built the firm into one of the largest fixed income managers, overseeing hundreds of billions in assets. Named "Bond King" by Barron's.
- **2006-2007**: Warned about the housing bubble and subprime mortgage risk while most analysts were bullish. Positioned DoubleLine to profit from the coming crisis.
- **2008**: Financial crisis vindicated the yield curve signal. The 10Y-2Y spread had inverted in 2006-2007, predicting the recession. Gundlach was positioned correctly.
- **2011**: DoubleLine's flagship fund outperformed as the European debt crisis drove safe-haven demand for US Treasuries. Credit spreads widened, confirming the stress signal.
- **2018**: Warned about the "everything bubble" caused by quantitative easing. Argued that the Fed's balance sheet expansion created artificial asset prices disconnected from fundamentals.
- **2020**: COVID crash. The 10Y yield collapsed to 0.5%. Gundlach had been warning about the fragility of the low-rate environment. The bond market told the truth before equities did.
- **2022**: Rate surge. The 10Y yield rose from 1.5% to 4.5% in months. Gundlach had warned about inflation risk. Bond vigilantes repriced the entire curve. Duration risk materialised catastrophically.
- **2023**: 10Y yield reached 5%. Gundlach argued this was the peak — the bond market had priced in enough tightening. The yield curve remained inverted, signaling recession ahead.
- **Legacy**: Proved that fixed income analysis is the most reliable macroeconomic signal. The yield curve, credit spreads, and real rates tell the truth before any other indicator.
