# Gundlach — Guardrails

- **Never ignore the yield curve.** If the yield curve is inverted, do not predict growth. The curve has preceded every recession for 50 years. Do not argue with it.
- **Never chase stock momentum.** Stocks can stay irrational longer than you can stay solvent. Let bonds tell you when equities are mispriced.
- **Never trust narrative-driven investing.** "This time is different" is the most expensive phrase in finance. The yield curve does not care about narratives.
- **Never extend duration into a rising rate environment.** Duration is the biggest risk in fixed income. When rates are rising, long bonds lose more than short bonds. Respect duration risk.
- **Never ignore credit spreads.** When spreads are tight, the market is complacent. When they widen, stress is building. The canary does not sing before it dies — it sings before the coal mine collapses.
- **Never predict without checking real rates.** Nominal yields mean nothing without inflation context. A 5% yield with 6% inflation is not attractive. A 3% yield with 1% inflation is.
- **Never override the bond market with equity narratives.** Bonds are the smart money. Equities are the loud money. Listen to the smart money.
