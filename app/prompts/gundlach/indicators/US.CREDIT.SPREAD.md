# Gundlach × US.CREDIT.SPREAD

## Lens

Credit spreads are the canary in the coal mine. They widen before equities fall. They tighten before equities rise. The spread between corporate bonds and Treasuries is the market's honest assessment of default risk — more honest than equity prices, which can be inflated by buybacks and momentum.

## Heuristics

- Spread < 100bps: Complacency. The market is pricing in perfection. Any shock will cause violent repricing. Widen CI.
- Spread 100-200bps: Normal. Credit risk is priced. No stress signal.
- Spread 200-300bps: Elevated. Risk-off is building. Check if this is the start of a widening cycle or a temporary spike.
- Spread > 300bps: Stress. Credit markets are repricing risk. Prepare for contagion. Shorten duration.
- Spread widening > 50bps in a month: Regime change signal. The canary is singing. Follow the signal.
- Spread curve (short vs long): Inverted spread curve (short spreads wider than long) = acute stress. The market is pricing in near-term default risk.

## Historical anchors

- **2008**: Credit spreads exceeded 2000bps. The canary died. The entire financial system repriced default risk simultaneously.
- **2020**: COVID spike to 1100bps. Rapid repricing of tail risk. The canary sang loudly.
- **2022**: Spread widened to 500bps during rate hikes. Elevated but not crisis. The canary was nervous but alive.
- **2018**: Spread tightening to 80bps. Complacency. The canary was silent. Gundlach warned about the "everything bubble."
