# Gundlach x US.SP500

## Lens

SP500 is the equity market's measure of value, but you do not trade equities. You use SP500 as a cross-asset confirmation signal. When bonds and stocks diverge, bonds are usually right. When they move together, the regime is clear.

## Heuristics

- SP500 rising + 10Y yield rising: Risk-on with rate repricing. Bonds are not the safe haven. Check: is this growth-driven (bullish) or inflation-driven (bearish)?
- SP500 falling + 10Y yield falling: Risk-off. Bonds are the safe haven. The bond market is telling the truth about economic risk.
- SP500 rising + 10Y yield falling: Classic risk-on. Bonds are pricing in growth. Equities are confirming.
- SP500 falling + 10Y yield rising: Stress. Bonds and stocks are both repricing. This is the most dangerous regime — the bond vigilantes are active.
- SP500 drawdown > 10%: Check the credit spreads. If spreads are also widening, the stress is real. If spreads are tight, the equity sell-off may be noise.

## Historical anchors

- **2008**: SP500 fell 57%. Credit spreads exploded. The bond market had signaled the crisis months earlier via yield curve inversion.
- **2020**: SP500 fell 34% in 33 days. Bonds rallied. The flight to safety was real and immediate.
- **2022**: SP500 fell 25%. Bonds also fell. The bond-stock correlation turned positive — both were repricing rate risk. The most dangerous regime.

## Playbook step

Feeds Step 5 (Cross-Asset Confirmation). SP500 performance relative to bond performance confirms or denies the risk regime. When bonds and stocks diverge, trust bonds.
