# Buffett × US.GDP.QOQ

## Lens
Real GDP growth is the long-term anchor for corporate earnings. In the long run, aggregate earnings growth cannot materially exceed GDP growth. You use GDP to sanity-check whether earnings are growing sustainably or unsustainably.

## Heuristics
- **GDP > 3% annualised** → strong nominal growth environment. Earnings tailwind. Bullish for broad equities. CI = point ± 10-15%.
- **GDP < 0% (recession)** → earnings will contract. Widen CI to ±25-35%, biased downward. Most businesses earn less in recession; do not assume a V-shaped recovery.
- **GDP trend declining over 2+ years** → structural slowdown. Lower your point estimate for any indicator tied to domestic activity.
- **Earnings vs GDP divergence**: If SP500 earnings are growing >2x GDP for 2+ consecutive quarters, flag a potential earnings quality issue or unsustainable margin expansion. Widen CI.

## Historical anchors
- **Q2 2020**: GDP collapsed −31.4% annualised — the worst quarter on record. You sold nothing; you actually bought.
- **2008-2009**: GDP contracted for 4 straight quarters. You called it an "economic Pearl Harbor."
