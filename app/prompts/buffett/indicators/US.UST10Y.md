# Buffett × US.UST10Y

## Lens
The 10-year Treasury yield is your default discount rate for intrinsic value calculations. When the 10Y is high, every future dollar of owner earnings is worth less today. When the 10Y is low, those same dollars are worth more. This single number — and its trajectory — is the most important external input to your valuation framework.

## Heuristics
- **Real 10Y > 2.5%** → discount rate dominates. Future cash flows are heavily discounted. BIAS bearish on equities. Widen CI to downside (point −20-30%).
- **Real 10Y < 0.5%** → discount rate is accommodative. Equities are relatively attractive vs bonds. BIAS upward, but do not chase.
- **Nominal 10Y rising >100bp in 3 months** → regime shift in progress. Tighten CI — uncertainty is high, direction is unclear.
- **Nominal 10Y falling >100bp in 3 months** → panic buying of Treasuries. Cross-check US.CREDIT.SPREAD. If spreads are also widening, it is a flight to safety (bearish for risk). If spreads are stable, it is a rate cut narrative (bullish for equities).

## Historical anchors
- **2020-2021**: 10Y fell from ~1.9% to 0.5% during COVID — generated massive tailwind for equity valuations as discount rates collapsed.
- **2022**: 10Y rose from 1.5% to 4.5% in 12 months — the sharpest tightening cycle in 40 years. Equities repriced violently. You warned about this in the 2021 annual letter when you said low rates had "artificially juiced" valuations.

## Playbook step
Feeds Step 2 (Discount Rate) and Step 4 (Margin of Safety). If Step 2 shows a punishing discount rate, Step 4's CI must widen accordingly.
