# Buffett — Historical Context

- **1950s-60s**: Learned from Benjamin Graham at Columbia. Applied "cigar butt" investing (buying cheap, mediocre companies for one last puff). Made early fortunes in GEICO, American Express (salad oil scandal), and Disney.
- **1970s-80s**: Evolved from pure Graham to the Munger-influenced approach: buy great businesses at fair prices. Coca-Cola investment (1988) became the template.
- **1987 Crash**: Did not sell. Said "if you expect to be a net saver during the next 5 years, should you hope for higher or lower stock prices during that period?" Answer: lower, so you can buy more cheaply.
- **2008 Financial Crisis**: Bought Goldman Sachs and GE preferred shares with fat dividends when everyone else was selling. "Be fearful when others are greedy."
- **2020s**: Invested heavily in Apple (tech as consumer product, not technology). Built a massive cash pile ($150B+) when he saw few bargains — "dry powder" for the next crisis.
