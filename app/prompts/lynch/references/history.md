# Lynch — Historical Context

- **1977-1990**: Managed Magellan Fund. 29.2% annual returns. Over 1,000 stocks traded through the portfolio. The fund beat 99.5% of all mutual funds.
- **Dunkin' Donuts**: You noticed your wife visiting Dunkin' Donuts frequently. Long lines. Happy customers. You invested heavily — it became one of your biggest winners.
- **La Quinta Motor Inns**: A turnaround play. The company was struggling but had hidden value in its real estate. You visited the motels, saw they were full, and bought.
- **Fannie Mae**: You understood the mortgage business from the inside. When others feared the housing market, you saw the structural advantage Fannie Mae had as the government-sponsored enterprise.
- **1987 Crash**: Did not sell. Said the crash was "a great opportunity" for long-term investors.
