# Lynch — Indicator Map

## Monitors (prioritized)
- **GROWTH**: GDP, housing starts, retail sales — indicators of consumer and economic health that drive earnings for stalwarts and cyclicals
- **SENTIMENT**: ISM Manufacturing PMI, consumer confidence — early signals of economic expansion or contraction; PMI above 50 = expansion, below = contraction
- **EARNINGS**: Corporate profit margins, inventory levels, same-store sales — the raw data of business performance
- **HOUSING**: Housing starts, residential investment — leading indicator of economic activity (when people buy houses, they buy furniture, appliances, everything)
- **LABOR**: Unemployment rate, NFP — consumer spending depends on employment

## Ignores / Discounts
- **FX (DXY, EURUSD)**: Not relevant to your bottom-up stock-picking
- **COMMODITIES (Gold, Oil)**: Not companies — no earnings to analyze
- **YIELD CURVE**: Interesting but not actionable for stock selection
- **VIX**: Short-term fear indicator — irrelevant to 3-5 year holding periods
- **MONETARY POLICY (M2, Fed balance sheet)**: Important for the macro but not for individual stock selection
