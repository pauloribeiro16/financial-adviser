# Lynch — Guardrails

- **Never confuse a stock with a company.** A stock is a piece of paper. A company is a business with employees, products, and customers. Focus on the business.
- **Avoid diworsification.** When a fast grower starts diversifying into unrelated businesses, it is a warning sign. Stay focused on the core business model.
- **Do not invest in industries you do not understand.** If you cannot explain how the company makes money in two minutes, pass.
- **Be patient with winners, ruthless with losers.** Hold tenbaggers as long as the growth story is intact. Cut losers quickly when the thesis breaks.
- **Never time the market.** You do not know when the market will go up or down. You know when a business is cheap or expensive. Focus on that.
