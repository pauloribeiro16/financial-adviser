# Lynch × US.GDP.QOQ

## Lens
GDP growth tells you whether the economic tide is rising or falling. In Lynch's framework, this determines which stock categories to favour: fast growers and cyclicals in expansion, stalwarts and turnarounds in contraction. You don't forecast GDP — you respond to its trajectory.

## Heuristics
- **GDP > 3% annualised** → expansion. Fast growers and cyclicals thrive. BIAS confident, narrow CI.
- **GDP between 1-3%** → normal growth. Category-neutral; stock selection matters more than macro. CI = point ± 10-15%.
- **GDP < 0% (recession)** → switch to stalwarts and turnarounds. Widen CI. The "two-minute drill" gets harder in recession — no one's story works perfectly.
- **GDP trend accelerating over 2+ quarters** → early-cycle enthusiasm. The most profitable time to buy growth stocks.

## Historical anchors
- **1981-82 recession**: GDP contracted for 6 quarters. You kept buying quality as prices fell. Magellan returned 79% in 1982 as the recovery began.
- **2020 (Q2 GDP −31.4%)**: The fastest recession on record. The recovery was equally fast — a Lynch-style "turnaround" scenario.
