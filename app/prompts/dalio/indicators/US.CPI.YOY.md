# Dalio × US.CPI.YOY

## Lens
CPI determines which macroeconomic paradigm we are in. Inflationary environments require different portfolios, different analytical approaches, and different mental models than deflationary ones. Getting the paradigm wrong is the most dangerous mistake an investor can make.

## Heuristics
- **CPI > 4%** → inflationary paradigm. Real assets outperform financial assets. Cash is trash. Bonds are dangerous. Assessments must account for rising nominal values across all indicators. Signal direction should bias upward for nominal-denominated indicators.
- **CPI 2-4%** → moderate inflation. Normal paradigm. Policy rates adjust in both directions. Assessments can be balanced.
- **CPI < 2%** → low inflation / deflation risk. The deflationary paradigm. Cash is king. Bonds are attractive (they rise in deflation). Assessments should be cautious — deflation is harder to escape than inflation.
- **CPI trajectory**: The direction matters more than the level. CPI transitioning from 2% to 5% = paradigm shift in progress. Lower signal_strength — the old rules are breaking. CPI falling from 8% to 3% = paradigm shifting back to normal. The worst is likely over.

## Historical anchors
- **1970s (CPI double-digit for most of the decade)**: The most extreme inflationary paradigm in modern US history. Commodities, gold, and real estate boomed. Equities were flat for a decade.
- **2008-2020 (CPI mostly < 2%)**: The deflationary paradigm that followed the GFC. Quantitative easing was necessary because the economy kept threatening to slip into deflation. Bonds rallied for 30 years.
- **2021-2023 (CPI surged from 1.4% to 9.1%)**: A paradigm shift from deflationary to inflationary in 18 months. Most investors were caught positioned for the old paradigm.

## Playbook step
Feeds Step 1 (Identify the Paradigm). The paradigm determines every other decision in the playbook.
