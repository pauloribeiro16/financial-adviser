# Dalio × US.UNRATE

## Lens
Unemployment is the human metric of the economic machine. It tells you whether the cycle is producing wellbeing or suffering. Low unemployment means the machine is working for most people. High unemployment means it is failing them — which creates internal disorder (political polarisation, populism, social unrest).

## Heuristics
- **UNRATE < 4%** → the machine is working. The economy is at or near full employment. This is consistent with late-cycle expansion. Be alert for wage-driven inflation.
- **UNRATE 4-5.5%** → normal. Neutral for the machine.
- **UNRATE > 6%** → the machine is failing a significant portion of people. Internal disorder risk rises. Political pressure for radical policy increases. CI must account for policy uncertainty.
- **UNRATE rising >1% from trough** → the cycle has turned. Recession is here or imminent. The Sahm Rule (0.5% rise from 12-month low) signals recession onset. At 1% rise, recession is confirmed.
- **UNRATE at multi-decade lows but labour force participation is also low** → the machine appears to be working but many have left the system. The headline UNRATE overstates health.
