# Dalio × US.GDP.QOQ

## Lens
GDP is the machine's output. It measures how much the economy is producing. In Dalio's framework, GDP growth is the result of the short-term debt cycle (demand driven by credit expansion/contraction) layered on top of the long-term debt cycle (the trend of productivity and debt accumulation).

## Heuristics
- **GDP > 3% annualised** → the machine is running hot. If this is accompanied by rising CPI and tightening labour markets, the short-term debt cycle is in its late expansion phase. Enjoy it while it lasts — recession follows expansion.
- **GDP 1-3%** → normal growth. The machine is ticking over.
- **GDP < 0%** → recession. The short-term debt cycle has turned contractionary. The question is whether this is a normal cycle contraction or the start of a long-term deleveraging (which would involve credit spreads >200bp and central bank intervention).
- **GDP growth declining for 2+ quarters while BS.FED is still expanding** → the machine is not responding to monetary stimulus. This suggests the economy is in a liquidity trap or structural constraint (deleveraging). Widen CI dramatically.
- **GDP vs total debt growth**: If debt is growing twice as fast as GDP, the long-term debt cycle is building. If GDP is growing faster than debt, the deleveraging is working.

## Playbook step
Feeds Step 2 (Short-Term Debt Cycle — GDP tells you whether the machine is expanding or contracting).
