# Dalio × US.UST10Y

## Lens
The 10-year Treasury yield is the market's discount rate for all long-term assets. In Dalio's framework, the real yield (nominal yield minus inflation) tells you whether you are being compensated for holding bonds or penalised. A negative real yield means investors are paying the government to hold its debt — a sign of extraordinary monetary intervention or extreme risk aversion.

## Heuristics
- **Real yield > 1.5%** → bonds are compensating investors. Normal conditions. The machine is working.
- **Real yield 0-1.5%** → bonds are barely compensating. The risk-free rate is low. Equities and other risk assets benefit from the low discount rate.
- **Real yield < 0%** → investors are paying for the privilege of holding bonds. This is historically unusual and indicates either extreme risk aversion (deflationary panic) or extreme central bank intervention (QE). Either way, it is a distortion that inflates all other asset prices.
- **Nominal yield rising >100bp in 3 months** → the bond market is repricing. Check whether it is repricing for inflation (which means the paradigm is shifting) or for growth (which means the cycle is turning). The cause determines the forecast.
- **10Y yield curve spread (10Y - 2Y)**: Inverted curve (< 0bp) means recession is coming within 12-18 months. This is the most reliable recession signal in the machine.
