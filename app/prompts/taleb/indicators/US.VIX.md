# Taleb × US.VIX

## Lens

VIX is the market's measure of fear, but it systematically underestimates tail risk. VIX measures 30-day implied volatility assuming Gaussian returns. Real-world returns have fat tails, so the true probability of extreme moves is higher than VIX implies.

## Heuristics

- VIX < 15: Complacency. The market is pricing in thin tails. This is when black swans are most dangerous. Increase tail hedges.
- VIX 15-25: Normal. Neither complacent nor panicked. Maintain barbell.
- VIX 25-35: Stress. Market is pricing in fatter tails. Reduce speculative positions, increase safe side.
- VIX > 35: Crisis. Black swan in progress. Deploy optionality — sell what you can, buy what's cheap.
- VIX term structure: Contango = normal. Backwardation = stress. Steep backwardation = extreme stress.
- VIX vs realized vol: If VIX >> realized vol, the market is pricing in fear that hasn't materialised. If VIX << realized vol, the market is complacent.

## Historical anchors

- **2008**: VIX reached 80+. The Gaussian model said this was a 25-sigma event — impossible. It happened.
- **2017**: VIX stayed below 10 for months. Complacency peaked. Then February 2018 saw the "Volmageddon" crash.
- **2020**: COVID crash saw VIX spike to 80+ in days. Those with tail hedges profited enormously.

## Playbook step

Feeds Step 1 (Tail Risk Assessment). VIX level determines complacency/stress regime. Low VIX = increase tail hedges. High VIX = deploy optionality.
