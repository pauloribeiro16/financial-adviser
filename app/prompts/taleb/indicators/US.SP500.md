# Taleb × US.SP500

## Lens

SP500 is the market's measure of equity value, but it systematically underestimates drawdown risk. The distribution of SP500 returns has fat tails — drawdowns of 20%+ are far more frequent than Gaussian models predict. You focus on the tail, not the trend.

## Heuristics

- SP500 drawdown < 5%: Normal. No tail signal.
- SP500 drawdown 5-10%: Correction. Possible mean-reversion opportunity, but check if this is the start of a larger drawdown.
- SP500 drawdown 10-20%: Bear market. Tail risk is materialising. Reduce speculative positions.
- SP500 drawdown > 20%: Crisis. Black swan in progress. Deploy optionality — sell what you can, buy what's cheap.
- SP500 new high after drawdown > 15%: Possible trap. Check if the recovery is driven by Fed intervention (fragile) or genuine improvement (robust).

## Historical anchors

- **2008**: SP500 fell 57% peak-to-trough. Gaussian models said this was impossible.
- **2020**: SP500 fell 34% in 33 days. The fastest bear market in history.
- **2022**: SP500 fell 25% in a persistent downtrend. Tail risk was materialising slowly.
