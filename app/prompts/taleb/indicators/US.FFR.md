# Taleb × US.FFR

## Lens

The Fed funds rate is the anchor of all asset pricing, but it is also the Fed's primary tool for creating and destroying fragility. Low rates create fragility (moral hazard, reach for yield, leverage). High rates create stress (debt servicing, recession risk). The Fed is damned if it does and damned if it doesn't.

## Heuristics

- FFR < 2%: Extremely loose. Fed is creating fragility. Moral hazard is high. Build tail hedges.
- FFR 2-4%: Normal. Neither creating nor destroying fragility.
- FFR > 4%: Tight. Fed is reducing fragility but increasing stress. Monitor for breakage.
- FFR > 6%: Extremely tight. System is under severe stress. Tail risk is elevated.
- FFR direction: Rising = reducing fragility, increasing stress. Falling = increasing fragility, reducing stress.

## Historical anchors

- **2008-2015**: FFR at zero for 7 years. Created massive fragility and moral hazard.
- **2022-2023**: FFR rose from 0% to 5.5% in 18 months. Created stress (SVB collapse).
- **2020**: FFR cut to zero overnight. Created fragility.

Feeds Step 1 (Tail Risk Assessment) and Step 2 (Fragility Scan). FFR level and direction determine monetary policy regime and systemic fragility.
