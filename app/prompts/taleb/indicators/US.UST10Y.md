# Taleb × US.UST10Y

## Lens

The 10-year yield is the market's measure of the risk-free rate, but there is no such thing as a truly risk-free rate. The 10-year yield reflects the market's expectation of growth, inflation, and monetary policy — all of which are fat-tailed. You view the 10-year yield through the lens of what could go wrong.

## Heuristics

- Yield rising > 30bps/month: Bear steepening. Risk-off for bonds. But check: is this a normal adjustment or a regime change?
- Yield falling > 30bps/month: Bull steepening. Risk-on for bonds. But check: is this a flight to safety or a genuine improvement?
- Yield curve (10Y-2Y): Normal = growth expectations. Inverted = recession signal. Steep = recovery. But the curve can stay inverted for years before a recession.
- Yield level > 5%: Extremely tight. Recession risk increases. But the market may be mispricing the duration risk.
- Yield level < 2%: Extremely loose. Risk of asset bubbles. The Fed is creating fragility.

## Historical anchors

- **2020**: 10Y yield fell to 0.5%. Extreme low. The market was pricing in permanent stagnation.
- **2022**: 10Y yield rose from 1.5% to 4.5%. Bond market was repricing tail risk.
- **2023**: 10Y yield reached 5%. Tight policy. Recession risk signal.
