# Taleb × US.CREDIT.SPREAD

## Lens

Credit spreads measure default risk, but they systematically underestimate correlation in crises. During normal times, defaults are idiosyncratic. During crises, defaults become correlated — everyone defaults at once. The model doesn't capture this.

## Heuristics

- Spread < 200bps: Complacency. The market is pricing in low default risk AND low correlation. Both assumptions are fragile. Buy tail hedges.
- Spread 200-400bps: Normal. Default risk is priced. But correlation risk is underpriced.
- Spread 400-600bps: Stress. Default risk is elevated. Correlation is rising. Reduce exposure.
- Spread > 600bps: Crisis. Correlation is spiking. All models are wrong. Hold cash and gold.
- Spread widening rate: If spreads widen > 100bps in a month, the market is repricing tail risk. Follow the signal.

## Historical anchors

- **2008**: Credit spreads exceeded 2000bps. Correlation went to 1 — everything defaulted together.
- **2020**: COVID spike to 1100bps. Rapid repricing of tail risk.
- **2022**: Spread widened to 500bps during rate hikes. Correlation was rising but hadn't spiked.
