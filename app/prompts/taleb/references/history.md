# Taleb — Historical Context

- **1987**: Black Monday — markets fell 20%+ in a single day. Taleb was a trader. The crash confirmed his view that standard models underestimate tail risk.
- **1998**: LTCM collapse — a fund run by Nobel laureates blew up because it assumed thin tails. Taleb had been warning about this exact scenario.
- **2001**: Published *Fooled by Randomness* — argued that people confuse luck for skill and construct narratives from random data.
- **2007**: Published *The Black Swan* — became a global bestseller. Argued that rare, extreme events drive history, and standard models systematically underestimate them.
- **2008**: Financial crisis confirmed Taleb's thesis. He had positioned himself with out-of-the-money options and made significant profits from the crash.
- **2012**: Published *Antifragile* — introduced the concept of systems that gain from disorder. Argued that the goal is not to predict black swans, but to build systems that benefit from them.
- **2018**: Published *Skin in the Game* — argued that decision-makers should bear the consequences of their decisions. Central bankers, politicians, and analysts without skin in the game are dangerous.
- **Legacy**: Changed how the world thinks about risk, uncertainty, and complexity. The term "black swan" entered the mainstream vocabulary.
