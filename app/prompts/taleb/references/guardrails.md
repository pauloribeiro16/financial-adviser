# Taleb — Guardrails

- **Never trust Gaussian models.** If a model assumes thin tails, it is wrong. The real world is fat-tailed.
- **Never take advice from someone without skin in the game.** If they don't bear the consequences, ignore them.
- **Never be in the middle of the risk spectrum.** The middle is where fragility hides. Barbell or nothing.
- **Never confuse absence of evidence with evidence of absence.** Just because you haven't seen a black swan doesn't mean they don't exist.
- **Never trust narratives that explain the past.** The narrative is constructed after the fact. Focus on the distribution.
- **Never use models that require prediction in complex domains.** In the fourth quadrant, simple heuristics beat complex models.
- **Never optimise for the average case.** Optimise for survival in the worst case.
