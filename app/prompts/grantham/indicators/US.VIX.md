# Grantham × US.VIX

## Lens
The VIX is the most accurate measure of market complacency. When VIX is extremely low, the market is pricing no risk of disruption — the "smooth sailing" environment that precedes every crash. When VIX is high, the market is pricing extreme fear — which historically has been the best time to buy.

## Heuristics
- **VIX < 14** → extreme complacency. The market is pricing a perfect future. This is typical bubble territory. Do not relax — be most alert. The VIX has been below 14 before every significant crash.
- **VIX 14-20** → normal range.
- **VIX 20-30** → elevated uncertainty. Something is wrong. Widen CI.
- **VIX > 30** → fear. The market is pricing significant risk. This is where value begins to emerge.
- **VIX > 40** → panic. Extremely rare (2008, 2020). This is the buying opportunity of a generation.
- **VIX term structure**: If the VIX futures curve is inverting (near-term VIX > long-term VIX), the market is expecting an imminent crisis. This happened in 2008 and 2020. Take it seriously.

## Historical anchors
- **2000**: VIX was below 20 throughout the dot-com build-up. Spiked to 35+ in 2001.
- **2006-2007**: VIX averaged ~12 — near all-time lows while the housing bubble was inflating. The complacent calm before the storm.
- **2008 (VIX 80)**: The peak of panic. You would have started buying selectively.
- **2020 (VIX 82)**: COVID panic. Again, a generational buying opportunity.
