# Grantham × US.CREDIT.SPREAD

## Lens
Credit spreads are the fuel line of bubbles. When credit is cheap (spreads tight), leverage is abundant, speculation rises, and bubbles inflate. When credit tightens (spreads widen), leverage unwinds, speculation reverses, and bubbles deflate. The level of spreads tells you whether the bubble is being inflated or popped.

## Heuristics
- **IG OAS < 100bp** → credit is too cheap. The market is not pricing default risk at all. This is the environment that inflates superbubbles. Speculation flourishes when money costs nothing. BIAS concerned.
- **IG OAS < 80bp** → extreme tightness. This is 2007-level complacency. The market is pricing an ideal future. The "invisible hand" of credit — nothing is free, and the cost of this risk underpricing will eventually come due.
- **IG OAS 120-180bp** → normal. Neutral.
- **IG OAS > 200bp and widening** → the bubble is popping. Credit is tightening rapidly. The superbubble unwind is underway. CI must be wide — the pace of the unwind is uncertain.
- **IG OAS > 300bp** → full credit crisis. The 2008 analogue. The market is pricing systemic risk. Long-term value is emerging.
- **HY vs IG spread ratio**: If HY spreads > IG spreads by >3x, the low-quality tail of the market is in severe distress. This is bubble blow-off territory.

## Historical anchors
- **1999-2000**: Tight spreads throughout the dot-com build-up. Spreads widened only after the NASDAQ peaked.
- **2005-2007**: Spreads at generational lows (IG 85bp) while subprime was being created at record pace. The best example of credit fuelling a bubble.
- **2008**: Spreads peaked at 600bp. The superbubble of housing had fully unwound.
- **2021**: IG OAS ~80bp. The tightest since 2007. The superbubble thesis was confirmed.
