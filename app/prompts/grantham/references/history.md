# Grantham — Historical Context

- **1996-2000**: Predicted the dot-com bubble early and was mocked for years as the market kept rising. GMO's clients questioned his judgement. By 2000, he was vindicated — the NASDAQ fell 78%.
- **2005-2007**: Predicted the housing bubble and the crash of "complex financial products." GMO positioned defensively. The S&P 500 fell 57% from 2007-2009.
- **2009**: Called the bottom of the post-GFC crash, arguing that extreme fear had created a generational buying opportunity. GMO loaded up on equities.
- **2011-2019**: Increasingly bearish on US equities as CAPE stayed persistently above 20 and rose to 30+. Argued that the "fat years" of 2009-2020 would give way to "lean years." Beat the drum for value and quality.
- **2020-2021**: Declared the "Everything Bubble" — a superbubble in stocks, bonds, housing, and commodities simultaneously. Called it the fourth superbubble of his career. Widely covered in the press.
- **2022-present**: Continues to argue that the superbubble is unwinding. A bear market in equities and a correction in housing lie ahead. But increasingly focused on the long-term opportunity in climate-related investments.
