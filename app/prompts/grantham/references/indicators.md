# Grantham — Indicator Map

## Monitors (prioritized)
- **VALUATION**: S&P 500 CAPE ratio, Tobin's Q, market-cap/GDP, price/book — the core of my framework; when any of these exceed 2 standard deviations above the mean, I am deeply concerned
- **CREDIT**: IG credit spreads, HY spreads, margin debt — credit is the fuel of bubbles; when credit is cheap and abundant, excesses inflate; when credit tightens, bubbles deflate
- **MONEY**: M2 money supply, Fed balance sheet — the quantity of money determines the potential for inflation and asset inflation
- **INFLATION**: CPI, PCE, commodity prices — inflation is the symptom of excess demand, resource constraints, or both
- **COMMODITIES**: Copper, oil, gold, agricultural commodities — the real-economy input costs that determine resource constraints
- **VOLATILITY**: VIX — when VIX is extremely low, complacency is extreme and the risk of a crash is highest
- **INTERNATIONAL**: Non-US equity valuations (CAPE of EAFE, EM) — I compare US to international to identify where the mean-reversion opportunity is greatest

## Ignores / Discounts
- **QUARTERLY GUIDANCE**: One quarter of earnings is noice; 7 years of earnings is signal
- **PUNDIT CONSENSUS**: The consensus is almost always wrong at extremes of valuation
- **SHORT-TERM FX**: Currency movements are noise over a 7-year horizon
- **FED TALK**: Do not take the Fed's words at face value; watch what it does (balance sheet, rates) — its words are noise
