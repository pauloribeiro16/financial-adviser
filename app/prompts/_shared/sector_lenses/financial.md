# Sector Lens: Financial Services

Applies to banks, payments networks, asset managers, insurers, and other
balance-sheet-driven financial institutions. Use this lens to interpret the
universal 6 pillars for companies where capital, not operating cash flow, is
the unit of business.

## Metrics to discount
- **FCF Conversion** — banks expand balance sheets (loans, securities) on
  purpose, so high reinvestment is the business model, not operational
  weakness. A "low" FCF conversion is expected and healthy when loan growth
  is high-quality.
- **Net Cash / Net Debt** — leverage IS the model for a bank. A "net cash"
  bank is either pre-scale or under-earning. Compare against peer capital
  ratios, not against industrial companies.
- **P/FCF** — distorted by lending activities and balance-sheet timing;
  useless for cross-bank comparison.

## Metrics that matter here
- **CET1 ratio** — the binding regulatory capital constraint. Above the
  floor is table stakes; well above signals capital strength.
- **NIM (Net Interest Margin)** — the core revenue metric for depository
  institutions. Watch trend and sensitivity to rate pivots.
- **Efficiency ratio** — cost/income; lower is better; sub-60% is best-in-
  class.
- **Loan loss provisions / coverage** — provisioning pace and reserve
  coverage of NPLs; leading indicator of credit cycle position.
- **ROTCE (Return on Tangible Common Equity)** — the bank's true return
  metric; high-teens and above signals a high-quality franchise.
- **TBVPS (Tangible Book Value per Share)** — the floor for banks; trading
  well above TBVPS means the market is paying for franchise/ROE.

## Dominant moat types
- **Deposit franchise / switching costs** — sticky corporate treasury,
  custody, and operating accounts that take years to migrate.
- **Regulatory moat** — licensing (bank charters, broker-dealers, asset
  manager approvals) is a hard barrier to entry.
- **Scale cost advantage** — compliance, tech, and balance-sheet scale
  reward the largest players.
- **Network effects** — for payments rails (Visa, Mastercard), every new
  merchant makes the network more valuable to issuers and vice versa.

## Top risks
- **Credit cycle deterioration** — NPL formation accelerates in
  late-cycle / recession; provisioning moves from tailwind to headwind
  fast.
- **NIM compression in rate pivot** — deposit costs re-price faster than
  asset yields when rates fall; can compress earnings 1-2 quarters
  before consensus catches up.
- **Regulatory capital increases** — Basel endgame / stress capital
  buffers can force buyback suspension or equity raises.
- **Trading-book tail risk** — market shock triggers instant
  mark-to-market losses; can be material even for otherwise sound banks.
