# Sector Lens: Technology

Applies to software (SaaS, infrastructure, platforms), semiconductors,
hardware, and large-cap internet / cloud. Use this lens when the business
is defined by intangible assets, recurring revenue, and platform dynamics
rather than physical asset turnover.

## Metrics to discount
- **Net Debt** — most large tech companies are net cash; the metric is not
  informative for cross-comparison. Look at gross leverage and duration
  of cash burn instead.
- **P/FCF (in isolation)** — can be extreme for high-growth SaaS; pair
  with growth and margin durability before drawing conclusions.

## Metrics that matter here
- **Rule of 40** — revenue growth rate + FCF margin should exceed 40%
  for healthy SaaS / cloud economics.
- **Gross margin** — pricing power; durable sub-60% software gross margin
  is a red flag; expanding GM is the strongest pricing-power signal.
- **R&D intensity (R&D / revenue)** — for tech, R&D is capex; track trend
  and absolute spend for the long-cycle moat.
- **SBC as % of revenue** — dilution is a real cost; persistent SBC >15%
  of revenue destroys per-share value even with reported earnings growth.
- **FCF margin (not absolute)** — scale matters; FCF margin expansion
  trajectory is more telling than the FCF dollar number.
- **Revenue growth durability** — look for cohort retention, NRR (net
  revenue retention), and the durability of the underlying platform.

## Dominant moat types
- **Network effects** — marketplaces and social platforms get more
  valuable with each user (think exchange effects, two-sided
  marketplaces).
- **Switching costs** — enterprise software and integrated ecosystems
  create deep customer lock-in (data gravity, workflow integration).
- **Counter-positioning** — new cloud-native or AI-native models that
  incumbents cannot easily replicate without cannibalizing their own
  franchise.
- **Intangibles** — IP portfolios, brand, and developer mindshare are
  real moats in tech.

## Top risks
- **AI disruption** — foundation models commoditizing adjacent layers
  (search, CRM, support, design) can collapse valuation premiums
  overnight.
- **SBC dilution** — especially dangerous pre-profitability; check the
  share count trend and the SBC line in the cash flow statement.
- **Antitrust / regulatory** — platform regulation in the US/EU
  (app-store rules, data privacy, content liability) can constrain the
  business model directly.
- **Key-person / founder dependency** — many tech franchises still hinge
  on a small number of executives; succession risk is non-trivial.
- **Platform decay** — switching-cost moats erode as APIs and data
  portability improve; legacy platforms lose pricing power.
