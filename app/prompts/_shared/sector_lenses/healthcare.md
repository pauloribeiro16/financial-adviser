# Sector Lens: Healthcare

Applies to pharmaceuticals (big pharma, biotech), medical devices, and
healthcare services (payors, providers, distributors). Use this lens when
the business is regulated, IP-driven, and subject to long, lumpy
development cycles.

## Metrics to discount
- **Revenue smoothness** — M&A close dates and milestone payments make
  pharma revenue look "lumpy" even when the underlying business is
  steady. Don't read volatility as operational weakness without checking
  the source.
- **FCF (in isolation)** — one-time licensing deals, contingent
  consideration, and royalty timing can distort FCF; pair with
  operating cash flow ex-milestones.

## Metrics that matter here
- **R&D productivity** — pipeline depth, phase progression, and
  probability-adjusted NPV of late-stage assets. The single most
  important tell for pharma.
- **Patent cliff timeline** — drugs losing exclusivity in the next 3-7
  years drive a step-down in revenue. Map it explicitly.
- **FCF yield** — for mature pharma / devices, FCF yield is the cleanest
  valuation lens; low yields usually signal either growth re-investment
  or peak-EBITDA risk.
- **Operating margin ex-R&D** — strips out the R&D reinvestment choice
  and shows the franchise's underlying profitability.
- **Segment mix** — innovative vs generic, biologics vs small molecule,
  US vs ex-US. Mix drives margin and growth durability.

## Dominant moat types
- **Intangibles** — patents, FDA approvals, and exclusivity windows
  (orphan drug, biologic 12-year) are the primary moat in pharma.
- **Regulatory** — FDA barriers (Phase I/II/III, 510(k), PMA) create
  years of defensibility and high failure costs for would-be entrants.
- **Scale distribution** — for devices and pharma, established sales
  and provider-relationship networks are hard to replicate.
- **Switching costs** — implanted devices (pacemakers, hips) and
  hospital-embedded systems create long-tail revenue with very low
  churn.

## Top risks
- **Patent cliff** — revenue can drop 30-80% in the year of generic
  entry for a blockbuster. Map expirations on a per-asset basis.
- **Clinical trial failure** — binary outcomes; a single Phase III
  failure can cut market cap in half.
- **Drug-pricing regulation** — IRA Medicare negotiation, drug
  importation, and international reference pricing compress peak
  sales.
- **Reimbursement risk** — CMS coverage decisions, payor formulary
  changes, and procedural denials can hit devices and services.
- **Litigation / product liability** — opioid settlements, device
  recalls, and off-label marketing suits create contingent liabilities.
