# Sector Lens: Energy

Applies to oil & gas supermajors, E&P independents, oilfield services, and
midstream (pipelines, terminals). Use this lens when the business is a
commodity price-taker where the cycle, not the operator, drives most of
the variance in reported numbers.

## Metrics to discount
- **Earnings** — commodity-driven and volatile. Headline EPS reflects
  the price of oil/gas far more than operational quality. Use
  normalized or strip-price earnings instead.
- **Margins** — swing with the commodity price; a tight margin in an
  up-cycle can be a worse signal than a wide margin in a down-cycle.
- **P/E ratio** — cycle-dependent. At the top of the cycle, P/E looks
  cheap because earnings are inflated; at the bottom, P/E looks rich
  because earnings are depressed. Both readings invert the truth.

## Metrics that matter here
- **Reserve replacement ratio (RRR)** — must be sustainably above 100%
  to maintain production; below 100% is a slow-motion decline.
- **FCF yield at strip (mid-cycle)** — the cleanest valuation lens; use
  mid-cycle prices, not spot.
- **Cash production cost per barrel** — the cost-advantage moat in $/bbl;
  the lowest-cost producers survive the worst downturns.
- **ROCE (Return on Capital Employed)** — capital efficiency through the
  cycle; high-cycle ROCE means disciplined capex.
- **Debt / Capitalization** — leverage through cycles matters more than
  point-in-time leverage; cycles stress balance sheets brutally.
- **Capex flexibility** — the ability to throttle capex down 30-50%
  quickly is the single best predictor of surviving downturns.

## Dominant moat types
- **Cost advantage** — low lifting cost is the primary moat; a
  $20/bbl producer wins every $50/bbl price war.
- **Reserve base** — resource quality and decline rates determine
  long-run production economics; tier-1 acreage compounds.
- **Midstream infrastructure** — pipelines and terminals create
  fee-based, take-or-pay revenue that decouples from commodity price.
- **Scale** — capex amortizes over more production; large supermajors
  can run projects smaller operators cannot finance.

## Top risks
- **Commodity price volatility** — oil/gas prices can halve in 6 months
  and the business model is a price-taker; this is the dominant risk.
- **Energy transition** — stranded asset risk accelerates as
  capital-markets and policy turn against fossil capex; reserve
  valuations can compress.
- **Reserve depletion** — running out of inventory is a slow, terminal
  risk; the asset base is literally being extracted.
- **Environmental / regulatory** — methane rules, carbon pricing,
  pipeline permits, and ESG capital restrictions can each add
  material cost.
- **Capex inflation** — service-cost inflation erodes project economics
  even at flat commodity prices; discipline breaks down in up-cycles.
