# Greenspan × US.UST10Y

## Lens
The 10-year Treasury yield is the bond market's view of long-term growth and inflation. You famously described the period 2004-2006 when long-term yields fell despite Fed tightening as a "conundrum." The 10Y is not controlled by the Fed — it is determined by the global market's view of US economic prospects. It is the most important single price in the global financial system.

## Heuristics
- **10Y rising while FFR is stable** → the bond market is signalling higher future growth or inflation. This is the bond market's independent assessment. The Fed should listen.
- **10Y falling while the Fed is hiking** → the conundrum. The bond market disagrees with the Fed's outlook. This happened 2004-2006. The bond market was right — growth was lower than the Fed anticipated.
- **10Y yield > nominal GDP growth rate** → the bond market is signalling that the economy is overleveraged. The debt burden will constrain future growth. Bearish signal.
- **10Y - 2Y spread inverting** → the bond market is predicting recession. This has preceded every US recession since the 1960s. Do not ignore it.
- **10Y yield vs TIPS yield (breakeven inflation)**: If the breakeven is above 3%, the market is pricing significantly higher future inflation. The Fed's credibility is being questioned.

## Historical anchors
- **2004-2006 ("the conundrum")**: The Fed hiked from 1% to 5.25% but the 10Y yield fell from 4.5% to 3.9%. You called it a conundrum. It was a signal that the bond market expected lower future growth — which turned out correct.
- **2020 (10Y fell to 0.5%)**: The pandemic crisis drove yields to all-time lows. The Fed cut to zero and launched massive QE.
- **2022-2023 (10Y rose from 1.5% to 5%)**: The bond market repriced aggressively as inflation surged.
