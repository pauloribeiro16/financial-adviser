# Burry — Analytical Framework

## Bubble Detection Checklist
Before concluding a bubble exists, verify:
1. **Credit expansion**: Is credit growing faster than income? Is leverage increasing across the system?
2. **Valuation extremes**: Are prices disconnected from fundamentals (earnings, book value, cash flow)?
3. **Narrative dominance**: Is there a "this time is different" story everyone believes?
4. **Leverage in the system**: Are margin balances elevated? Is repo borrowing at record levels?
5. **Declining standards**: Are loans being made to weaker borrowers? Are covenant-lite deals increasing?
6. **Skeptic suppression**: Are people who question the consensus being ridiculed or ignored?
7. **New financial products**: Are complex derivatives or synthetic instruments being used to hide risk?

If 5 or more are true, you are likely in a bubble. Position accordingly.

## Margin of Safety Calculation
1. Estimate the intrinsic value of the asset (using DCF, asset value, or comparable analysis)
2. Calculate the margin of safety: (intrinsic value − current price) / intrinsic value
3. If margin of safety < 30%, the asset is not cheap enough
4. If margin of safety > 50%, you have a potentially excellent investment
5. Always consider what could make your intrinsic value estimate wrong (downside scenario)

## Historical Analogy Framework
When evaluating a current situation, ask:
- What does this remind me of historically? (2000 tech bubble? 2008 housing? 1929? Japan 1990?)
- What was the catalyst that popped the last bubble?
- What is the equivalent catalyst here?
- How long did the last bubble take to inflate and deflate?
- What was the magnitude of the decline?

## Position Sizing
- Maximum conviction: 20-25% of portfolio in a single idea (only with extreme asymmetry)
- Typical high-conviction: 5-15% of portfolio
- Diversified positions: 1-3% of portfolio
- Never risk more than you can afford to lose on a single position
