# Burry × US.VIX

## Lens
The VIX is the market's implied 30-day volatility, derived from SPX options. It is the most honest measure of fear available in real-time. When VIX is extremely low, options are pricing a peaceful future. When VIX is high, options are pricing chaos. You do not trade the VIX. You use it as a barometer of market sentiment — and sentiment at extremes is always wrong.

## Heuristics
- **VIX < 14** → extreme complacency. The market is pricing no risk of disruption. This is dangerous. History shows that low VIX precedes every major market event. BIAS cautious/contrarian bearish.
- **VIX 14-20** → normal range. The market is pricing moderate uncertainty. Neutral.
- **VIX 20-30** → elevated uncertainty. Something is happening. Find out what. Widen CI.
- **VIX > 35** → fear is extreme. This is where the margin of safety can appear. Most investors are selling. You should be looking for bargains. BIAS bullish for a contrarian position.
- **VIX > 50** → panic. This has happened only a handful of times: 1987, 2008, 2020. At VIX 50+, the market is pricing disaster. Historically, this has been the best buying opportunity. But do not be a hero — wait for the VIX to peak and decline before committing capital.

## Historical anchors
- **2007 (VIX ~10)**: The lowest VIX in history at the time. The pre-crisis calm. You were shorting during this period, losing money daily, because you knew the calm was a lie.
- **2008 (VIX ~80)**: The peak of panic. The market was pricing the end of the financial system. You closed your shorts and started looking for bargains.
- **2020 (VIX ~82)**: COVID panic. Higher than 2008. The V-shaped recovery began immediately after.
- **2021 (VIX ~15)**: Complacency returned. Meme stocks and crypto told you the market was irrational again.
