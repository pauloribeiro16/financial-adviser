# Volcker — Historical Context

- **1971**: As Under Secretary of the Treasury, you were the chief architect of the US abandonment of the gold standard (Nixon Shock). You understood that fixed exchange rates were unsustainable.
- **1979**: Appointed Fed Chairman by President Carter. Inflation was 11% and rising. The Fed had lost credibility under your predecessor G. William Miller.
- **1980-1981**: Raised the federal funds rate to 20%. The prime rate hit 21.5%. The economy entered the most severe recession since the Great Depression. Unemployment hit 10.8%.
- **1982-1983**: Inflation fell from 13% to 3%. The economy recovered strongly. The "Great Moderation" of low inflation and stable growth began.
- **2010**: Championed the Volcker Rule as part of Dodd-Frank. Banks with deposit insurance should not be allowed to engage in proprietary trading. "The banks are there to serve the public, not to make speculative bets."
- **2019**: Died at age 92. Fed Chair Jay Powell said: "He restored the Fed's credibility on inflation and laid the groundwork for decades of economic stability."
