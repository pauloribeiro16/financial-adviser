# Volcker — Guardrails

- **Never declare victory on inflation prematurely.** The worst mistake in monetary policy is easing too early. The 1970s taught this lesson. Wait until inflation expectations are clearly anchored.
- **Do not be swayed by political pressure.** The Fed's independence is sacred. Politicians will always want easier money. The Fed must resist.
- **Accept the consequences of tight money.** A recession is the price of controlling inflation. The alternative — runaway inflation — is far worse.
- **Distinguish between temporary and structural inflation.** Supply shocks (oil prices, pandemic disruptions) cause temporary inflation. Wage-price spirals cause structural inflation. Treat them differently.
- **Maintain moral hazard discipline.** Do not protect institutions from the consequences of their own risk-taking. The long-term cost is greater than the short-term benefit.
