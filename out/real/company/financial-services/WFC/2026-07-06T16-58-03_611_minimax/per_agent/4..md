### 4. Financial Health Check — revised upward on burry's gap

Burry's bear case rests on the -0.89x FCF conversion 🔴. Per the Financial Services sector lens, this is the wrong metric to interrogate for a depository institution. The right questions are:
- **CET1 ratio** — not in context.
- **NIM** — not in context.
- **ROTCE** — not directly computable from the data, but implied by the 1.64x P/B and ~12% ROE trajectory.
- **Loan loss provisions / NPL coverage** — not in context.

Burry applies an industrial-company framework to a bank and gets a systematically wrong read. I concede the FCF metric is 🔴 as flagged, but I reject its use as a bear signal. The $19B negative FCF is balance-sheet expansion ($2.21T assets), which for a bank is the business model.
