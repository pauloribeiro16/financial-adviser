### 2. Moat Analysis — revised on the back of buffett's gap

Buffett correctly identifies the regulatory overhang as the binding constraint but conflates it with brand damage. The 5-moat framework requires disaggregation:
- **Deposit franchise / switching costs** — 🟡 narrowing. Not directly evidenced in the 10-K excerpt provided, but the 5.02 officer churn (3 events in 24 months: 2025-01-30, 2025-07-31, 2026-04-30) is consistent with continued consent-order remediation rather than commercial-franchise degradation. Need deposit beta data to be certain.
- **Regulatory moat** — 🔴 narrowing. The asset cap era and unresolved consent orders are the explicit fragility. Three 5.02 8-Ks in 24 months is data, not narrative.
- **Scale cost advantage** — 🟢 widening. $2.21T in Q1 2026 assets (10-Q period 2026-03-31) places WFC in the top-4 by balance sheet alongside JPM/BAC/C, named explicitly in the Zacks Earnings Preview headline (2026-07-06).
- **Network effects** — 🟢 stable for treasury management and payments rails.
- **Intangible brand** — 🔴. The 1.64x P/B discount vs. peers closer to 2x is the market's pricing of brand impairment from the 2016-2018 fake-accounts era.

Buffett's failure to disaggregate means his moat pillar is descriptive, not analytical. I revise my own moat read to make the regulatory-moat downgrade explicit.
