### Points of disagreement
- Whether Beta 0.261 is "defensive" (Dalio, Buffett) or "anomalously low and misleading — low-beta stocks fall as hard as high-beta in commodity downturns" (Burry, Simons)
- Whether the fortress balance sheet "survives $40 oil" (Buffett) or whether that claim ignores committed Encino development capex and assumes unrealistic capex cuts (Eisman, Burry)
- Whether ROIC 13.7% is structurally sub-benchmark (Dalio) or cyclically depressed to ~16-17% at mid-cycle strip (Burry, Eisman)
- Whether Net Cash $-5.0B (🔴) is a meaningful red flag (Gundlach, Simons) or a sector-context labeling error since Net Debt/EBITDA is 0.40x (🟢) (Dalio, Eisman, Burry)
- Whether capex at $6.59B in 2025 represents "transitory deal-related" spending (Buffett) or "empire-building-into-the-cycle-top" pattern that destroyed E&P balance sheets in 2014-2016 (Burry, Eisman)
- Whether the forward P/E of 8.9x vs trailing 13.0x is a "warning of further earnings compression" (Dalio) or "embedded ~32% earnings growth expectation" representing a torque-on signal (Simons)
- Whether the macro overlay (10Y at 4.06%, IG-HY OAS at 4.13%) is "restrictive/bearish" (Gundlach) or "benign low-vol regime supportive of carry" (Simons); Burry notes the FRED OAS data is dated 2023-07-10, questioning its current relevance
- Whether Encino Utica is "reserve depth at fair value" (Simons, Eisman) or "value-destructive buying at the cycle top with debt" (Burry)
