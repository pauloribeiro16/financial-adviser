### 1. Business Lifecycle (Cycle-Lens Reframe)

All five personas correctly identify MPC as **Capital Return** with cyclical overlays. The cash flow data confirms: $36.17B repurchased 2022-2025 against $37.07B FCF (Cash Flow statement). But the cycle-lens reframes this — capital return at cycle-peak (EBITDA $24.88B in 2022) is appropriate; capital return at cycle-decompression (EBITDA $11.68B in 2025, -53% from peak) while debt is rising ($34.36B total debt 2025 vs $27.91B 2022, Net Debt +61%) is dangerous. The Q1 2026 net income collapse to $511M (Net Income Loss line, 10-Q period ending 2026-03-31) vs FY2025 $4.05B (full year) — annualized ~$2B against a $78.7B market cap — confirms the cycle has rolled. **My view shifts from Capital Return-appropriate to Capital Return-during-late-cycle compression.**
